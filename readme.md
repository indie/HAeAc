#Silencing the Cessnas

No other neighborhood in the #Portland metro area has as much nuisance air #pollution and noise pollution traffic 
as do we in little Forest Grove, Oregon. I started compiling this data a long time ago, and myriad more now compounding 
the glaring facts.

When you live more than 15 miles away from a small airport, what is the likelihood that small aircraft fly over your 
block more than 8 times a day ...  10 times a day ... a dozen or more?  

Videos, pictures, a whole entire repository of time-stamped [screencaptures] like these, showing that #Hillsboro Aero 
Academy treats the airspace over Forest Grove like a playground.  Not Lake Oswego.  Not Beaverton.  Not Scappoose.  They 
seem to explicitly avoid flying over every other populated area in the whole valley except ours.

I first noticed the issue about ~ 2 years,  and shortly after that I wrote a complaint to the #FAA about noise pollution 
and airplanes flying too frequently OVER MY BLOCK and too low over the giant #sequoia trees in my neighborhood.  I have this 
complaint copied on one of my many Linux partitions from years ago, gotta go dig it up soon.

I can't do anything in my yard when there are airplanes circling over my house.  My yard shows the neglect, too.  I have 
suffered intense emotional, mental and physical distress.  You may have noticed how much I post about airplanes? 

This is not what I want to be doing.

The planes were more infrequent back before I complained.  This could be intentional, or a "coincidence" related to the  
[backlash against the expansion] of the airport itself, while out-of-state "businessmen" try their damnedest to avoid 
accountability where they live and work and send their kids to school.  

In either case, the noise has increased, so  I'm putting my talent in the pool.   My #AI talent, my descriptive words 
like razors that cut through oodles of bullshit, my research skills honed to a precision of 5 nanometers, which I trust 
Intel to measure.

Sometimes 15 times a day do these parades of toxic 1977 Cessnas, 1979 Cessnas, 1981 Cessnas.  Seminoles fly overhead, 
choking out toxic diesel fumes and noise.   

This is tedious., stressful work but I will get compensated for each and every airplane pass that flies overhead.  

All screencapture were initiated from sound alone. I hear it and capture it, $???,??? per violation, to be distributed 
among my comrades.  We ask for a lot,  because we deserve it.  

And we'll use this as a #model to help other people in our community and other communities that might not be in Oregon.

By the way, the owner of the #Hillsboro Aero Academy is not an #Oregon resident.  He does not pay taxes here.  He 
bribed someone to take the #ACT test for his kid to get a better score for University.   Went to prison for it, too.  
Is this who we want to be suffering for? 

Someone pumping his bank account full of money from young wannabe pilots while harassing all the people, birds and wildlife 
in a state he does not even live in!

The frequency of flights increased after I used the FAA Federal government tool to make the complaint, and this will be 
a key aspect of the lawsuit.  

*In the meantime* ... I'm taking all the data to the police station, too, to file a restraining order.   

If you live in this area, I encourage you to do the same.   Use my data, or capture your own, we can be collab buddies 
on this repo.  We'll use advanced math to map the average flight paths, where they cross most.   

Juries LOVE this kind of nuance.

Note that my complaints are specifically against Hillsboro Aero Academy, not the airport itself.  We can keep the Hillsboro 
airport, the airshow where ONCE per year, people expect and delight in airplane noises ... but we're going to shut down 
Hillsboro Aero Academy.
